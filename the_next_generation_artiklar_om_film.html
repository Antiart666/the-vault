THE NEXT GENERATION Between 1987 and 1992, Jarl shot a third installment called The Social Contract. It was originally conceived to involve Kenta and Stoffe's two sons, Patrik and Jan. However, Stoffe's widow, Carina, didn't like the idea, and Jarl decided to focus on Kenta's family instead. Kenta once again steps into the picture, looking old and worn. Ironically his son, Patrik, has adopted the Yuppie lifestyle of the eighties. In a turn of events that no one could have predicted, both Jan and Patrik avoid the self-destructive traps that dragged their fathers down and both get themselves the "decent life" their fathers tried to obtain. The Misfit trilogy is a remarkable living panorama of Society and its social outcasts. It is an important work of film art, and maybe even Harry Schein would now agree that the Trilogy is the work of an Author. As to the question of whether misfits can have a decent life, the answer must be that they can, but that trying to obtain it while hooked on hard drugs leads nowhere. Kenta is living proof that misfits can have a decent life. Or, as he says in The Social Contract: ”When I was twenty I gave society the finger. Now I'm about to turn fifty and I still do it - but behind their backs.” RUSS MEYER: THANKS FOR THE MAMMARIES!
