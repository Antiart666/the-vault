Stockholm film festival 1999or Am I Experienced Now?

Titel: Stockholm film festival 1999or Am I Experienced Now? So, I have at last attended a filmfestival. I never thought it would happen. Being a single father, too much work combined with lack of money, it's that kind of thing I've only dreamt about. But since my parents are great folks, they GAVE me money to go away a couple of days and really get into celluloid, lots of candy, even more beer and a diet consisting of fast food. And I don't even live at their place! Said and done. I stayed at my good friend Anders' apartment, where also Magnus, another good friend, stayed. Soon things went completely nutzoid. Not only did I see some great films: I also had the opportunity to, almost, appear in Entombed's (a great Swedish death metal band) new video. The video was directed by none the less than Troma leader Lloyd Kaufman. I shook hands with Annabelle (I-made-it-with-251-guys-in-ten-hours) Chong. I also got my picture taken with Dario Argento and finally drank a beer and socialized with Lloyd Kaufman. As I said, things went nutzoid. Let me tell you about it. The Entombed experience Since Magnus was working at The Stockholm Film Festival he knew what was happening where and when. So, after the screening of The Phantom of the Opera (see review) we hijacked a cab and sort of raced to the cinema where the Entombed video was going to be shot. We arrived at one a.m. and we knew that no less than eighthundred (800) invitations had gone public so we got nervous we couldn't get in, cause we were late! That was not the problem (it became apparent), because there were almost no one there. A very nervous L.G. Petrov (the singer in Entombed) asked us »who the hell are you? How come you're at the set?« Things didn't get more cheerful when Magnus said »Well, that's a secret«. Anyway, we didn't get thrown out of the perimeters (yet) and some more people arrived. After a lot of waiting (professional actors will say that that is exactly what moviemaking is about, a lot of waiting) we finally got to see Kaufman at work. That is for approximately three minutes. Then we were told that the only actors (!?) needed was going to be lesbians. Since I'm a man (even though several people have doubted that for many, many years) and my two musketeers also are male, we got kicked out in the cold. Hopefully the video will be great. Entombed + Toxic Avenger + lesbians = good video. All you smutpeddlers out there will recognize one or two Swedish pornostars among the lesbians (I didn't recognize them, I swear. This information was only given to me and I just pass it along). The Dario Argento experience After a cancelled interview that Magnus was going to make with Deborah Kara Unger (Crash), who supposedly is no way as nice as she looks. Tired and a bit disappointed we simply drank some very expensive coffee and had breakfast with Mr. Lloyd Kaufman (I know I'm bragging here, and face it: it's going to be even worse in a while). Then Dario Argento stepped into the cafe. Under the guidance of Ronny (I-know-a-lot-about-Dario-Argento) Svensson, Dario spoke to the small audience and answered some questions. Argento's English has improved over the years (while mine just gets worse and worse) and he turned out to be a witty and fun guy. Nevertheless, instead of talking about his movies, he just talked. And talked. He just had to tell us about a dinner the night before that the Stockholm Film Festival had held for their special guests. An older gentleman talked to Argento and said how much he loved his movies. But Dario knew! He just fucking knew, »This guy doesn't know who I am«. So Dario Argento convinced the older guy that he was a director from Chile, and not only that. He convinced the poor guy that he's the most famous porno director from Chile. He makes porno films with dogs, cows, pigs, bears and no the less than Roman Polanski steps forward and says to the old man that »Yes, he is the greatest pornodirector right now.« And when one fan was wondering about the third film of the »Three Mothers« (original, huh!) movie was going to be made Argento rather talked about the guy's Dawn of the Dead T-shirt. (For you who don't know. Suspiria and Inferno are the two first instalments in a planned trilogy. What they're all about I don't understand, you don't understand and I bet that not even Dario knows what the hell they're about. One thing is sure though, the two first films are among the most visually stunning films you'll ever see). He said some interesting stuff though. In his next movie the original Goblins will make the score and that he's going to use non-professional actors in all the roles. Argento doesn't think that there are any good actors out there any more. So instead he will use real prostitutes in the role of prostitutes, paupers as paupers and so forth. The question if the victim really is going to be played by a real victim, and the murderer really is going to kill the victim I held to myself. If Dario had to talk about good actors, he did name a few for himself: The flies in Phenomena, the rats in The Phantom of the Opera, and the maggots in Suspiria. Babe 3 anyone? He has gone completely bonkers! Dario also talked a little about his latest film The Phantom of the Opera. Since he admired the novel by Gaston Leroux and felt that the other dramatizations of it sucked, he wanted to do it justice (read review for answer to that particular question). And thanks to a very big (for him, not for Spielberg) budget, a nice Hungarian crew and his lovely daughter he thought that it was a good experience to do the film. The conference was over and it was time for us losers without a decent life to stand in line to get autographs, shake hands or have our picture taken with the man. To prove I'm a pathetic loser too, look at the enclosed pic for evidence. The Lloyd Kaufman experience At Saturday night the Stockholm Film Festival threw a respectable party with live acts and some DJ's. I was getting kind of drunk and obnoxious when I recognized someone standing in the crowd, Lloyd Kaufman! Would he remember me from the cafe? Would he speak to me? Was I still able to speak and stand up at the same time? The questions speeded through my brain, but before I realized it myself, I was talking to him. Mr. Lloyd Kaufman turned out to be a gentleman in any manner (if we exclude his films). He was talkative and polite and answered my questions and asked me some. It didn't take many minutes before I was babbling away like a baboon about Violent Vision and me being a filmteacher (yepp, that's true). Somehow he did not walk away from me (I know I would). So we spoke for a long time and before you can say »Klatou Berata Nektou« Violent Vision will be proud to present an interview with the Tromatic Kaufman. So I leave it here. The Annabelle Chong experience I've seen the infamous gang-bang film with Annabelle Chong and the revolting 251 guys. I also saw the interesting and tought-provoking documentary about her, SEX: The Annabelle Chong Story at the festival, so when she stood there at the party I knew I had to introduce myself (yeah, yeah, I know what you perverts think. I just wanted to say Hi!). Said and done. Magnus and I approached her. Annabelle's host at the festival, Linda, introduced us (Linda and Magnus are friends). So I shook hands, and I leaned towards her ear and screamed (the music was really loud at this time, or I was drunk), »And how are you enjoying your stay here in Stockholm?« She said »Fine!« and went along dancing with Linda. There is no meaning to mention this; I just wanted to. The experience of the experience I'm hooked. Next year I'll will have more money, more time and will attack more unawaring people. I will haunt every person that I'll find interesting, and do you know something: They're just like us! And if you're lucky, like I was this year, they're just as friendly as you and I.
