House On The Edge Of The Park

Titel: House On The Edge Of The Park
 If Wes Craven knew what he started with Last House on the Left, do you think he would had made it? Probably not, but you can't change what is already done so why bother? Anyway, of all the rip-offs of the original this must be considered as one of the best. Ruggero Deodato knew what he was doing when he let David Hess play the lead in a role even more sadistic than Krug. Even before the film starts Hess manages to rape and kill a woman. Good work Hess, don't you ever feel like you are typecasted? Anyway on Hess' right side we have the piss boy of Italian cinema, Mr John Morghen! Get this, a film with both Hess and Morghen can't fail. These two charming guys invites themselves to a party (or that's whay they think) and before you can say Wes Craven, things start to get nasty. Morghen starts things with making a fool of himself in front of everybody doing some strange disco dance and even Hess looks a bit embarresed. Under a card game things get real out of hand, ending with the beating of one of the guests. Now Hess wants to have the party his way. Y' know, rape and cutting. One great scene is when Hess throws a poor guy in the pool and starts to piss on him and laughs like hell. Don't try this at home. Morghen is not as mean though and really don't want to rape Lorriane De Selle (instead he falls in love with her). The truth about the party slowly comes forth. The girl Hess raped in the beginning was a sister to De Salle and she wants to have him dead. Now this is what we see on the surface of the film. But underneath there is a social statement going on! Director Deodato might not have thought about this, but we at VV has figured out a little thing (and I thought I never would analyze a film). Hess and Morghen is representing the working class and is being pushed about by these yuppie assholes. So, House on the Edge of the Park is really a sort of class statement! The strong, wealthy ones always pick on the working class, but in Deodato's way the lower class bites the hand that feeds them. Fucking great, but if you look at in that way the ending is kind of depressing. But if you are working class and got an invitation to a yuppie party, take my advise and see this film before you go! Who knows, you might get an idea or two.
