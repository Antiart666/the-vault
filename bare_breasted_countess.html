Bare Breasted Countess

Titel: Bare Breasted Countess 
Frankly my friends, I can't understand why there are a lot of you Jess Franco-addicts out there. Of course some people can't understand why I like films like Caligula-The Untold Story so maybe it will be best if I rest my case. Accept my humble excuse. Anyway Bare Breasted Countess is one of those films with endless Franco-zooming in and out of faces, bodies and pubic hair. Really. But who is complaining about that? Lina Romay, Mrs. Franco, plays a vampire chick who seems to have some problem with her own vampyricism, but just can't keep away from doing it anyway. She's mute and runs around in some strange transparent lingerie all of the time (can't more girls do that?). She stays on a hotel with her bodyguard, who also is a mute and who is kind enough to bring her with victims sometimes. Of course she likes both men and women, right in the tradition of all those other vampire babes from the early seventies. In one scene Romay is with two women in a basement. First Romay gets naked and is whipped a bit just for fun before she attacks and kills one of the women and drinks her blood. This was one of the few good moments in the film. Which I think sez quite a lot. Jess appears in this film, playing a long haired investigator and is very baddly dubbed. But who cares about that, aren't they all in this one? Romay on the other hand is a real beauty and when she twists around naked in a bathtube filled with blood in the ending it's getting hard to watch (get the joke?). Wonder if Ruggero Deodato got some inspiration from this scene when he made Charlotte Lewis take a bath in the very weird Dial Help (see review)? Jack Taylor, who has made more than one film with Franco (Succubus etc), plays a strange guy who shaves naked and reads books (sometimes at the same time). Jack claims that he didn't know that this one was going to be 'sort-of-a porno film' when he made it, but what would he expect? Sometimes Franco shines through as a stylish director with some inspired scenes, but the overall impression is kind of weak. A plus (or should it be a major minus?) for the ugly female journalist with the beergut. She made me more sick than the psychedelic music in this one. Give me Joe D'Amato instead!
