    A Blade in the Dark by Christer Persson Films which are made in a theatrical way are mostly good ones provided that the director knows what he is doing. Unfortunately Lamberto Bava doesn't really succeed here. A Blade in the Dark is set on one location practically all the time and the real tension will not appear because the house is not scary enough. Bava just doesn't exploit the settings in a satisfying way. This isn't a bad giallo, but it's a bit slow and a bit ill-defined. A young horrorfilm composer, played by Andrea Occhipinti (»Donald Duck« in New York Ripper), rents a big house to get some solitude when he is composing. Sure he get's! Girls (read Italian beauties) emerge from closets and bushes only to disappear in mysterious ways. Does he have a lively imagination or are the girls really killed in his house? Small signs like of blood and marks after knives surely points at it. And what significance has the strange »last reel« of the film got to do with everything? Who knows? As usual with Bava's pictures it's a pleasure for the eyes. The camera sweeps over floors and dead bodies. The best scenes are the ones involving murders and has that 'Argentotesque' kind of feeling. Very powerful indeed. Overall good performances and it's fun to see Michele Soavi in a leading role. The haunting score is by Guido and Maurizio De Angelis (any connections with Fabrizio?).Not Bava's best, nor his worst.
